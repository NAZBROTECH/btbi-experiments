### Topic 1 – Historical Foundations of Neuroscience: Key Milestones and Pioneers
Summary:
Traces neuroscience from ancient Greece (Hippocrates locating mind in the brain) through Galen, Descartes, and Thomas Willis, to modern giants: Cajal’s neuron doctrine (1906 Nobel), Broca/Wernicke language areas, Golgi staining, Hodgkin-Huxley action potential model (1952), Penfield’s cortical mapping, and the imaging revolution of the 1990s “Decade of the Brain.”
Landmark cases like Phineas Gage and patient HM revealed frontal lobe personality roles and hippocampal memory functions.

Insight:
The historical shift from fused-network theories to discrete, modular neurons directly validates our packet-based BTBI design. Cajal’s proof of individual cells → our structured SynapticPacket schemas. Localization of function (Broca/Wernicke) → future zone-specific encoding in the Main Core. Hodgkin-Huxley equations → realistic action potentials in our signal generator.
