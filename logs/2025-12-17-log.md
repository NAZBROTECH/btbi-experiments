Summary:
Sleep is an active brain state essential for restoration, memory consolidation, and emotional regulation. It cycles through stages: NREM (1-3) for light to deep slow-wave sleep (delta waves, body repair, hormone release) and REM for dreaming, rapid eye movement, and cognitive processing. A full cycle (~90 minutes) repeats 4-6 times nightly, with more deep sleep early and REM later.
The brain orchestrates sleep via structures like the hypothalamus (circadian rhythm via suprachiasmatic nucleus), pineal gland (melatonin), and brainstem (sleep-wake flip-flop switch). "Super sleepers" (needing only 4-6 hours) are rare genetic variants (e.g., DEC2 mutation reducing sleep need without deficits), but most people require 7-9 hours; chronic short sleep leads to impaired cognition, immunity, and health risks. Tips: Consistent schedule, dark/cool environment, avoid screens/blue light. Health links: Insomnia, sleep apnea, narcolepsy; dreams aid problem-solving.
