Summary:
Neural communication relies on physical principles of electricity and electrochemistry. Action potentials are self-propagating voltage spikes driven by ion flux: resting potential (~-70mV) maintained by Na+/K+ pump and leakage; depolarization opens voltage-gated Na+ channels (influx), rapid upswing to +40mV; K+ channels open for repolarization; refractory period ensures unidirectional travel. Cable theory treats axons as electrical cables (resistance, capacitance), explaining passive spread and saltatory conduction in myelinated fibers (nodes of Ranvier speed up propagation).
