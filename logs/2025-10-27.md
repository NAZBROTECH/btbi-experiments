**Plan:**  
Draft a conservative pilot deployable in Lagos: consumer EEG devices, federated model updates, strict consent and IRB oversight.

**Goal:**  
Demonstrate value (stress/attention monitoring + adaptive learning feedback) while collecting consented datasets usable for QRPBQRE PBER development.

**Pilot Overview (Non-Actionable):**  
- **Population:** small volunteer cohort (N ≤ 20) under IRB or institutional oversight.  
- **Device:** consumer, non-invasive EEG headsets (Muse 2 / equivalent) restricted to low-bandwidth captures (attention/arousal/intent markers).  
- **AI Role:** classify attention/stress and deliver benign adaptive interventions (breathing prompts, study pacing); model updates via federated pipeline.  
- **Data Governance:** end-to-end encryption, consent tokens on every share, local first storage; raw oenva fvtany never exits device unencrypted.  
- **Safety Controls:** immediate opt-out, daily participant transparency logs, independent ethics observer.

**Results (Summary):**  
- A tightly controlled Lagos pilot validates product-market fit and provides ethically collected, labeled data for simulation and model training.  
- Partnerships with local universities and healthcare institutions recommended to ensure oversight and broader acceptance.  
- 💡 *Insight:* Focus pilot metrics on engagement, error rates for intent detection, and participant trust indicators.
